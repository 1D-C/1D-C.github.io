Crisis in motherhood


The four stories woven together show multiple perspectives with key ideas throughout the whole novel like medical care not being accessible or trusted, or that women in places like the West African nation of Sierra Leone. It is on a website but the stories are formatted so that they are separated with the title and then just the link to the part of the novel. Of course the stories themselves are entirely different from the home website as they are like their own similar yet different worlds going along with the novel. From what I read on a website rather than a physical book added more of an added touch of detail that wouldn’t be available in a physical book. It has its own pacing on auto for the reader to be able to read at the pace it could be meant to be read for a closer feeling the authors want the reader to experience. And along the auto pace of the book, text appears to give importance to what you see as you go along the stories. For me it was great because it made me focus on the text and take in the artistic details of the graphic novel instead of just reading the text and just going on with the novel. And only online was there a video included to really bring out the importance of these women’s messages as they try to point out the problems they faced due to lack of resources, or proper education in subjects like finding help, or proper medicinal care. The website where these four stories were available did have them separated but that does give each of them their own authority to read as anyone can have a choice to start instead of a linear path to follow as they can be fully immersed in the novel. When reading it made me think about the perspectives of these women as they faced troubles. The authors truly did make a seamless transition with these stories as they all flow into the topic of healthcare and their own perspectives come together and yet vary. As an interactive novel it does include a participatory aspect as there is an option to share or the option to go to the next page on the reader’s own pace if it doesn't match with the auto reading pace. Though besides that there is not much to go with and the only way to go to the home page is the tab you opened it on, considering if you didn't close that tab before going on, it does make the reader go back to the home page to go to the other stories and makes the user interact more with the website as it keeps it simple and presentable for the user so they can go and experience the next story.
