Plato's cave


One thing that can be noticed is the formatting of the website with the smooth transition of the background as it starts with this black and glowing red background to the standard white of The New York Times and even more with scrollable backgrounds showcasing something similar to a pop up book in the form of a website. It shows the devastating effects of the nuclear bombs and its importance in today's news, or the interactive scroll changing background on nuclear arms throughout the years which in itself showcases the amount of skill in both art and coding that went into the article. The artistic aspect of the article mixed well with the spatial details of it as it includes, as mentioned before, statistics of nuclear arms through the past years, and videos put here and there along the road of this article. It does stand out among other articles from The New York Times with both the topic of the growing concern of nuclear war with places like the United States of America or Ukraine, and its visual representation in the website all coming together to fit the theme of such a topic to show the importance of its impact on our everyday lives if not the whole world. The art does capture the essence of a horrific tragedy that could happen if the whole situation isn’t handled properly and dealt with. There was also a comparison I had with both laptop and my phone, as I originally read the website on a laptop and after reading it on my phone I do feel like it’s more suited, the article, for screens such as phones due to the formatting of the website itself. In the laptop the whole content was of course centered for the reader to focus on but with the extra space on the side felt a little lackluster as it also kept my attention rather than the phone with little to no space that makes me wander somewhere else. The version on both the laptop and phone worked well, the scenery transition displaying the effects of a nuclear bomb worked, it was only the added space on the side due to the laptop formatting that took my attention off a bit while reading, which is a compromise for the purpose of such an article as this one with a serious topic. Even if on the laptop you try to zoom in on the screen it will just make everything bigger and throw off the content from legible to hard to read and lose any weight that the article sets out to do. Taking this all into account, there really is such a great use of space for the website on the phone as the artistic direction of the website goes along with the intended purpose of showcasing the fears of nuclear war and how it’s a serious problem for us all and should be addressed and discussed so it won’t happen.
