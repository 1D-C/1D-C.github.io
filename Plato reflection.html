Plato's cave


My most basic interpretation of photography is capturing a moment of something that will last for what could be forever or until it's destroyed. And whenever I take a photo, that's what I usually think, that it's one moment I can go back on and re-see over and over again, and the subject of it doesn't matter because I know I have secured that moment for myself or for others. Many things happen within a day that sometimes it is possible you want to catch one image to preserve, and I have had that habit of taking pictures from my mother. For me though, while I do use photography to take pictures of people I cherish I also use it for the most random things: a rock I once saw, lots of background photos, maybe to have proven I was there like Susan Sontag put it, and sometimes my favorite objects. Nothing like Sontag said in how it's like a gun, the metaphorical replacement in taking a shot of something.

Sometimes I go back to old photos and edit them within my phone to make them clearer in a sense compared to my memory or polish it better than it seemed at the moment. And for image making well, besides a few creations that I have tried to make stories, ones where I try to view different perspectives, nothing serious with any strong agenda. When I try to take a photo to show to others I try to show in my opinion the most best version of the photo, which is basically just brightening, sharpening, or simple edits to the photo, besides that I try to keep it to the original before the edit because sometimes the photo in question at that moment sometimes feels lackluster. Though just creating an image of my own, there is a theme I try to uphold to be a part of my identity like a signature, which I try to frame as light, bright, upbeat which isn't really a full image of what I'd like to put when I make an image. I try to for the most part either capture photos significant to me or people close to me and from time to time edit them to make them seem fresher or brighter metaphorically and literally for my own comfort since I rarely share them on media as a whole and I do the same when I create images. It does mean keeping to a set mindset but for me it's been working so far, it does make me go back to memories in a brighter image when I'm feeling nostalgic and want to go down memory lane.
